# Requirements Document

## Introduction

TAILORSTAQ is a multi-tenant SaaS platform built with Node.js (backend) and Vue.js (frontend) that enables professional tailors and tailoring businesses to operate their own branded online shops. Each tailor business (tenant) gets a dedicated shop within the platform where they can manage products, customers, and orders. The platform supports free and paid subscription tiers, a tenant approval workflow, customer order tracking, and branded communications (receipts and emails) using each shop's logo and contact information. The platform's brand identity uses white, dark blue, and chocolate brown as its primary colors.

---

## Glossary

- **Platform**: The TAILORSTAQ SaaS application as a whole.
- **Tenant**: A tailoring business (e.g., NE Clothiers) registered and approved on the Platform.
- **Shop**: The branded storefront belonging to a Tenant within the Platform.
- **Tenant_Admin**: The owner or administrator of a Tenant's Shop.
- **Customer**: An end user who creates an account on the Platform to place and track orders.
- **Platform_Admin**: A TAILORSTAQ staff member who manages tenant approvals, subscriptions, and platform-wide settings.
- **Subscription**: A billing plan (Free or Paid) that governs a Tenant's access to Platform features.
- **Order**: A tailoring service or product request placed by a Customer within a Shop.
- **Receipt**: A document generated by the Platform summarizing a completed or confirmed Order, branded with the Tenant's logo and contact information.
- **Product**: A tailoring service or item offered by a Tenant within their Shop.
- **Approval_Request**: A registration submission from a prospective Tenant awaiting review by the Platform_Admin.
- **Notification**: An email or in-platform message sent to a Tenant_Admin or Customer.

## Requirements

### Requirement 1: Tenant Registration and Approval

**User Story:** As a tailoring business owner, I want to register my business on TAILORSTAQ and await approval, so that I can set up my shop and start serving customers.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL provide a registration form that collects the prospective Tenant's business name (1–100 characters), contact email (valid RFC 5321 format), phone number (7–20 characters), and business description (1–500 characters).
2. WHEN a prospective Tenant submits a registration form with a contact email already associated with an existing Approval_Request or Tenant account, THE Platform SHALL reject the submission and display an error message indicating the email is already in use.
3. WHEN a prospective Tenant submits a valid registration form, THE Platform SHALL create an Approval_Request with a status of "pending" and send a confirmation Notification to the submitted email address.
4. WHEN a Platform_Admin reviews an Approval_Request, THE Platform SHALL allow the Platform_Admin to approve or reject the Approval_Request.
5. WHEN a Platform_Admin approves an Approval_Request, THE Platform SHALL create a Tenant account, assign the default Free Subscription, and send an approval Notification to the Tenant's registered email address.
6. WHEN a Platform_Admin rejects an Approval_Request, THE Platform SHALL update the Approval_Request status to "rejected", record the mandatory rejection reason (1–500 characters), and send a rejection Notification to the prospective Tenant's registered email address including the reason for rejection.
7. WHILE an Approval_Request has a status of "pending" or "rejected", THE Platform SHALL prevent the prospective Tenant from accessing any Shop management, product, or order features.
8. WHEN a Platform_Admin views the Approval_Request list, THE Platform SHALL display all Approval_Requests and allow filtering by status (pending, approved, rejected).

### Requirement 2: Tenant Shop Setup

**User Story:** As a Tenant_Admin, I want to configure my Shop with a name, logo, products, and contact information, so that my Shop reflects my brand and customers can reach me.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Tenant account is created, THE Platform SHALL provision an empty Shop associated exclusively with that Tenant.
2. THE Tenant_Admin SHALL be able to set the Shop name (1–100 characters), upload a Shop logo, provide a business address (1–255 characters), phone number (7–20 characters), and contact email (valid RFC 5321 format) for the Shop.
3. WHEN a Tenant_Admin uploads a Shop logo, THE Platform SHALL accept image files in PNG, JPG, or SVG format with a maximum file size of 5 MB.
4. IF a Tenant_Admin submits a Shop logo file that exceeds 5 MB, is 0 bytes, or is not in PNG, JPG, or SVG format, THEN THE Platform SHALL reject the upload and display a descriptive error message identifying the specific validation failure.
5. THE Tenant_Admin SHALL be able to add, edit, and remove Products within their Shop, where each Product includes a name (1–100 characters), description (1–1000 characters), price (0.01–999,999.99), and an optional image.
6. WHEN a Tenant_Admin uploads a Product image, THE Platform SHALL accept image files in PNG, JPG, or SVG format with a maximum file size of 5 MB, and SHALL reject files that exceed 5 MB, are 0 bytes, or are not in an accepted format with a descriptive error message.
7. WHEN a Tenant_Admin saves Shop settings, THE Platform SHALL persist the Shop name, logo, contact information, and Products and reflect the changes in the Shop within 5 seconds.
8. IF a Tenant_Admin attempts to access or modify a Shop that is not associated with their Tenant account, THEN THE Platform SHALL return a 403 Forbidden response and deny the operation.

### Requirement 3: Subscription Tiers

**User Story:** As a Tenant_Admin, I want to choose between free and paid subscription plans, so that I can access the features appropriate for my business size and budget.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL offer at least two Subscription tiers: a Free tier and a Paid tier, each with a defined set of feature entitlements.
2. WHEN a Tenant account is created, THE Platform SHALL assign the Free Subscription tier to the Tenant by default.
3. WHILE a Tenant holds a Free Subscription, THE Platform SHALL enforce the Free tier limits of a maximum of 10 active Products and 50 Orders per calendar month.
4. WHEN a Tenant_Admin on the Free tier attempts an action that would exceed the Free tier limits, THE Platform SHALL block the action, display a message identifying which limit was reached, and present an option to upgrade to the Paid tier.
5. WHEN a Tenant_Admin initiates an upgrade to a Paid Subscription, THE Platform SHALL present the price per billing period, the billing period duration, and a summary of Paid tier features, and SHALL require payment confirmation before activating the Paid Subscription.
6. IF a Tenant_Admin abandons the upgrade flow before payment confirmation, THEN THE Platform SHALL leave the Tenant on the Free Subscription tier and discard any partial payment data.
7. WHEN a Tenant_Admin's Paid Subscription payment is confirmed, THE Platform SHALL activate the Paid Subscription and send a confirmation Notification to the Tenant_Admin's email address.
8. WHEN a Paid Subscription expires or payment fails, THE Platform SHALL downgrade the Tenant to the Free Subscription tier and attempt to send a Notification to the Tenant_Admin; the downgrade SHALL proceed regardless of whether the Notification is successfully delivered.
9. WHEN a Tenant_Admin views the Shop dashboard, THE Platform SHALL display the current Subscription tier, the number of active Products against the tier limit, the number of Orders placed this calendar month against the tier limit, and available upgrade options.

### Requirement 4: Customer Account Management

**User Story:** As a customer, I want to create an account on TAILORSTAQ, so that I can place orders and track their progress across shops.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL allow any visitor to register a Customer account by providing a full name (1–100 characters), a valid RFC 5321 email address, and a password that is at least 8 characters long and contains at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one digit, and one special character.
2. WHEN a visitor submits a Customer registration form, THE Platform SHALL validate that the email address is unique within the Platform and send an email verification Notification containing a verification link to the provided address.
3. WHEN a Customer verifies their email address by following the verification link, THE Platform SHALL activate the Customer account and allow the Customer to log in.
4. IF a verification link has not been used within 24 hours of issuance, THEN THE Platform SHALL mark the link as expired, and WHEN the Customer attempts to use an expired link, THE Platform SHALL display a message indicating the link has expired and offer to resend a new verification link.
5. IF a visitor attempts to register with an email address already associated with an existing account, THEN THE Platform SHALL reject the registration and display a descriptive error message.
6. IF a Customer whose account has not yet been verified attempts to log in, THEN THE Platform SHALL reject the login and display a message instructing the Customer to verify their email address.
7. WHILE a Customer account has a status of "active", THE Customer SHALL be able to update their full name, email address, and password from their account profile.
8. WHEN a Customer updates their email address, THE Platform SHALL send a verification Notification to the new email address and SHALL NOT apply the email address change until the new address is verified; the Customer's existing email address SHALL remain active until verification is complete.

### Requirement 5: Order Placement and Tracking

**User Story:** As a customer, I want to place orders in a Shop and monitor their status, so that I know when my tailored items will be ready.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a logged-in Customer selects a Product in a Shop, specifies a quantity between 1 and 99, and submits an order, THE Platform SHALL create an Order associated with that Customer and that Shop, with an initial status of "received".
2. THE Platform SHALL assign each Order a unique Order reference number consisting of 8–12 uppercase alphanumeric characters at the time of creation.
3. THE Platform SHALL define the following Order status lifecycle: "received" → "in-progress" → "ready-for-pickup" → "completed", with "cancelled" available as a terminal status transition from any non-final status.
4. WHEN a Tenant_Admin updates the status of an Order, THE Platform SHALL record the new status and a UTC timestamp of the change, and SHALL attempt to send a Notification to the Customer; the status change SHALL be recorded regardless of whether the Notification is successfully delivered.
5. THE Customer SHALL be able to view a list of all their Orders across all Shops, including the Order reference number, Shop name, Product name, quantity, current status, and last updated timestamp.
6. THE Customer SHALL be able to view the full status history of any individual Order, showing each recorded status value and its associated UTC timestamp.
7. WHILE an Order has a status of "received", "in-progress", or "ready-for-pickup", THE Tenant_Admin SHALL be able to update the Order status to any valid next status in the lifecycle or to "cancelled".
8. IF a Tenant_Admin attempts to update the status of an Order that has a status of "completed" or "cancelled", THEN THE Platform SHALL reject the update and return a descriptive error message indicating the Order is in a terminal state.

### Requirement 6: Receipts and Branded Communications

**User Story:** As a Tenant_Admin, I want receipts and customer emails to display my Shop's logo and contact information, so that my customers associate communications with my brand.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN an Order status is set to "completed", THE Platform SHALL generate a Receipt for that Order.
2. THE Platform SHALL include the Shop's logo, Shop name, business address, phone number, and contact email on every Receipt.
3. THE Platform SHALL include the Order reference number, Customer name, Product name, quantity, unit price, line total, Order total, and Order completion date on every Receipt.
4. WHEN a Receipt is successfully generated, THE Platform SHALL attempt to send the Receipt to the Customer's registered email address as a PDF attachment; IF the email delivery fails, THEN THE Platform SHALL record the delivery failure and retain the Receipt so the Customer can download it.
5. THE Customer SHALL be able to download the Receipt for any completed Order from their order history as a PDF file.
6. WHEN the Platform sends any Notification email to a Customer on behalf of a Shop, THE Platform SHALL include the Shop's name and contact email in the email footer.
7. IF a Shop has not uploaded a logo at the time a Receipt is generated, THEN THE Platform SHALL substitute a default TAILORSTAQ logo placeholder on the Receipt.
8. IF a Shop's business address, phone number, or contact email is not set at the time a Receipt is generated, THEN THE Platform SHALL omit the missing field from the Receipt and display a "not provided" label in its place.

### Requirement 7: Multi-Tenancy and Data Isolation

**User Story:** As a Platform_Admin, I want each tenant's data to be strictly isolated, so that no tenant can access another tenant's shop data, customers, or orders.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL associate every Shop, Product, Order, and Customer-Shop interaction record (including order history and shop-specific Customer records) with exactly one Tenant identifier.
2. WHEN a Tenant_Admin makes an API request, THE Platform SHALL validate that the requested resource belongs to the authenticated Tenant before returning or modifying data.
3. IF a Tenant_Admin's API request references a resource belonging to a different Tenant, THEN THE Platform SHALL return a 403 Forbidden response, log the access attempt with the requesting Tenant identifier, the target resource identifier, and a UTC timestamp, and make the log retrievable by the Platform_Admin.
4. THE Platform SHALL ensure that Customer identity data (full name, email address, password) is stored at the platform level and shared across Shops, while Orders, order history, and shop-specific Customer interaction records remain scoped to the Tenant whose Shop received the Order.
5. WHEN the Platform executes a database query on behalf of a Tenant_Admin, THE Platform SHALL apply a Tenant identifier filter to all queries that access Shops, Products, Orders, and Customer-Shop interaction records.
6. IF a Tenant_Admin's API request attempts to read or modify Customer identity data beyond what is necessary to fulfill an Order within their own Shop, THEN THE Platform SHALL return a 403 Forbidden response and deny the operation.

### Requirement 8: Authentication and Authorization

**User Story:** As a platform user, I want secure login and role-based access, so that only authorized users can perform actions appropriate to their role.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL support three distinct roles: Platform_Admin, Tenant_Admin, and Customer, each with a defined set of permissions.
2. WHEN a user submits valid login credentials, THE Platform SHALL authenticate the credentials and issue a signed JSON Web Token (JWT) with an expiry of no more than 24 hours.
3. WHEN a JWT expires and the user makes a subsequent request, THE Platform SHALL reject the request with an authentication error response indicating the token has expired and require the user to re-authenticate.
4. IF a user submits invalid login credentials, THEN THE Platform SHALL return a generic authentication failure message and increment a failed-attempt counter for that account; WHEN the user subsequently submits valid credentials, THE Platform SHALL reset the failed-attempt counter to zero.
5. WHEN a user's failed-attempt counter reaches 5 consecutive failed attempts, THE Platform SHALL lock the account for 15 minutes, attempt to send a Notification to the account's registered email address, and automatically unlock the account and reset the counter after the 15-minute lockout period expires.
6. IF an unauthenticated request is made to a protected API endpoint, THEN THE Platform SHALL return a 401 Unauthorized response.
7. IF an authenticated user's role does not have permission for the requested action, THEN THE Platform SHALL return a 403 Forbidden response.
8. WHERE a Tenant_Admin is accessing Shop management endpoints, THE Platform SHALL additionally verify that the Tenant_Admin's Tenant identifier matches the Shop's Tenant identifier, and SHALL return a 403 Forbidden response if the identifiers do not match, even when the Tenant_Admin's credentials are otherwise valid.

### Requirement 9: Platform Administration

**User Story:** As a Platform_Admin, I want a dashboard to manage tenants, subscriptions, and platform health, so that I can operate TAILORSTAQ effectively.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. WHEN a Platform_Admin views the Tenant list, THE Platform SHALL display all Tenants with their business name, Subscription tier, registration date, and account status (active, suspended).
2. WHEN a Platform_Admin suspends an active Tenant account, THE Platform SHALL set the Tenant account status to "suspended", terminate any active sessions for that Tenant_Admin, prevent the Tenant_Admin from accessing Shop management features, and attempt to send a Notification to the Tenant_Admin; the suspension SHALL take effect regardless of whether the Notification is successfully delivered.
3. WHEN a Platform_Admin reactivates a suspended Tenant account, THE Platform SHALL set the Tenant account status to "active", restore the Tenant_Admin's access to Shop management features, and attempt to send a Notification to the Tenant_Admin; the reactivation SHALL take effect regardless of whether the Notification is successfully delivered.
4. IF a Platform_Admin attempts to suspend a Tenant account that is already suspended, or reactivate a Tenant account that is already active, THEN THE Platform SHALL reject the operation and return a descriptive error message indicating the account is already in the requested state.
5. WHEN a Platform_Admin views the platform metrics dashboard with a specified date range, THE Platform SHALL display the total number of Tenants, the count of active Subscriptions broken down by tier, and the total number of Orders placed within the specified date range.

---

### Requirement 10: Frontend Brand Identity

**User Story:** As a platform visitor or user, I want the TAILORSTAQ interface to reflect a consistent brand identity, so that the platform feels professional and trustworthy.

#### Acceptance Criteria

1. THE Platform SHALL render all public-facing and authenticated pages using white, dark blue, and chocolate brown as the primary color palette, applied consistently across backgrounds, primary actions, and accent elements.
2. THE Platform SHALL display the TAILORSTAQ brand name and logo in the top navigation bar on all pages.
3. THE Platform SHALL be responsive such that all page content is visible and usable without horizontal scrolling on viewport widths from 320px to 1920px, and no content overlaps or is clipped at any width within that range.
4. WHEN a page is loading data from the backend, THE Platform SHALL display a visible loading indicator that remains present for the full duration of the loading state.
5. IF a backend API request returns an error, THEN THE Platform SHALL display a human-readable error message that does not include a raw HTTP status code, stack trace, or internal error identifier.
